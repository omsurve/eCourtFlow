About the Ministry

Ministry of Law and Justice is the oldest limb of the Government of India dating back to 1833 when the Charter Act 1833 enacted by the British Parliament. The said Act vested for the first time legislative power in a single authority, namely the Governor General in Council. By virtue of this authority and the authority vested under him under section 22 of the Indian Councils Act 1861 the Governor General in Council enacted laws for the country from 1834 to 1920. After the commencement of the Government of India Act 1919 the legislative power was exercised by the Indian Legislature constituted thereunder. The Government of India Act 1919 was followed by the Government of India Act 1935. With the passing of the Indian Independence Act 1947 India became a Dominion and the Dominion Legislature made laws from 1947 to 1949 under the provisions of section 100 of the Government of India Act 1935 as adapted by the India (Provisional Constitution) Order 1947. Under the Constitution of India which came into force on the 26th January 1950 the legislative power is vested in Parliament.

Types of Law

Civil Law

Governs disputes between individuals or entities regarding rights and responsibilities of Citizens.

Family Law

Addresses legal matters related to family relationships, such as divorce and child custody.

Business Law

Concerned with legal aspects of commercial activities, contracts, and corporate governance.

Education Law

Deals with legal issues in the education sector, including student rights and school regulations.

Criminal Law

Criminal law is a legal framework that defines and regulates behaviors deemed harmful to society.

Cyber Law

Includes legal regulations and protections in the digital realm with online activities & cybersecurity.

Have A Questions?

1 What we do ?
Our SMART Algorithms prioritize cases based on their complexity and urgency, and assign them to the most appropriate judge. This ensures that cases are heard by the right judge at the right time, and that judges are able to focus on the most important cases.
2 What tasks can clerks perform in the DCM system?
Clerks have the responsibility of adding cases when all the necessary documents are available. They can also view and assign cases to the most available judge, ensuring that cases are distributed efficiently.
3 How do judges use the DCM system?
Judges can use the DCM system to view cases based on their severity. This allows them to prioritize and manage cases effectively, ensuring that urgent matters receive prompt attention.
4 Is the DCM system user-friendly and easy to navigate?
Yes, the DCM system is designed with user-friendliness in mind. It offers a straightforward interface for administrators, clerks, and judges, making it easy for them to perform their respective tasks.
